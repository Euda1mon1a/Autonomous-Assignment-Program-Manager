CRITICAL FINDINGS SUMMARY
================================================================================

1. Dual Model Implementation
   - NetworkX-based SIR (simple, transparent)
   - NDlib-based SIS (advanced, scalable)

2. Rt = 1.0 is THE Critical Threshold
   - Below 1.0: Burnout naturally declining
   - Above 1.0: Exponential growth (epidemic)

3. 80% Utilization Convergence
   - Queuing theory breaks down at 80%
   - R₀ crosses 1.0 around 80%
   - Phase transition alignment

4. Super-Spreader Leverage
   - 20% of faculty cause 80% of transmission
   - 1 super-spreader intervention prevents 10+ secondary cases

5. Early Warning Window
   - Syndromic surveillance catches cases 4-8 weeks early
   - Contact tracing identifies exposed 2-4 weeks before burnout

6. Network Topology Matters
   - Contact structure determines spread patterns
   - Small-world networks (medical teams): clusters + bridges

7. Herd Immunity Threshold
   - If >50% have capacity (load <30), system is protected
   - Strategic "vaccination": Protect hubs first

8. Hidden 30% Risk
   - Allostatic load captures ~70% of burnout drivers
   - Remaining 30%: moral injury, fairness, autonomy, meaning
