### Amion (Whoison / Spiral Software)
*Source: [Amion Order/Pricing Page](https://www.amion.com/order), [Amion for Enterprises PDF](http://mail.amion.com/images/Amion_for_Enterprises.pdf), [Today's Hospitalist article](https://todayshospitalist.com/off-the-shelf-scheduling/), Reddit anecdotes*

**Pricing Model: Per-Group (Per-Schedule), NOT per provider**

| Tier | Target | Price |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Residency** | Single residency schedule | ~**$349–$449/year** per group |
| **Pro** | Clinic/department/small team | ~**$349/year** per schedule (self-serve setup) |
| **Enterprise** | Hospital/health system | **$349/year × number of schedules** |
| Concierge add-on | Schedule build assistance | +$600/year per group |
| Active Directory/SSO add-on | Enterprise security | +$1,000/year |

**Key data points:**
- Amion serves **>200,000 providers**, **9,000 groups and residencies**, and nearly **200 entire hospitals/health systems**
- "**70%+ of teaching hospitals** in the U.S. have their biggest residency programs online with us" — Amion marketing document
- Historical pricing: $249/year (2010 article), ~$350-449/year current (Reddit 2023: "running like 350 bucks a year")
- A 200-bed hospital with 30 departments/groups: ~$10,470/year ($349 × 30 schedules)
- A large AMC with 100 schedules: ~$34,900/year

**Amion Revenue Estimate (rough):** If 9,000 groups × average $400/year = **~$3.6M ARR** for the group-only business; enterprise hospital contracts add a multiplier. With ~200 enterprise hospitals × average $15,000/year (30-40 schedules each) = ~$3M more. **Estimated total Amion ARR: $5-8M.** Very low ARPU but extremely high market penetration in residency programs.

## 5. Back-of-Envelope TAM Calculation

### Definitions
- **TAM (Total Addressable Market):** All residency/fellowship scheduling software spend if every program used dedicated software

- **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market):** Realistic capture with a focused product

## 6. Growth Trends & Regulatory Tailwinds

### Program & Trainee Growth
*Source: [ACGME 2024-2025 Annual Report](https://www.acgme.org/globalassets/pdfs/2024-2025acgmeannualreportforweb.pdf), [AAMC 2025 Report on Residents](https://www.aamc.org/data-reports/students-residents/data/report-residents/2025/executive-summary)*

- **13,762 programs in 2024-2025** vs. ~11,299 in 2015-2016: **+21.8% program growth over 10 years**
- **167,083 residents/fellows** vs. ~124,900 in 2014-2015: **+33.8% trainee growth over 10 years**
- Net **457 new programs added in 2024-2025 alone** (71 more than the prior year) — accelerating rate
- 2025 Match: 43,237 positions offered (largest ever, +4.2% YoY); 231 more certified programs; 877 more primary care positions
- AAMC projects **~4,000 additional active residents per year** going forward

### Resident Physician Shortage Reduction Act
*Source: [AAMC press release, December 2025](https://www.aamc.org/news/press-releases/distribution-400-new-medicare-supported-graduate-medical-education-residency-positions-marks), [AHA support letter, July 2025](https://www.aha.org/lettercomment/2025-07-07-aha-supports-bipartisan-resident-physician-shortage-reduction-act), [ACR legislative update](https://www.acr.org/News-and-Publications/legislation-introduced-to-bolster-physician-workforce)*

- **Resident Physician Shortage Reduction Act of 2025** (H.R. 4731/S. 2439): bipartisan legislation to add **14,000 new Medicare-supported GME positions over 7 years** (2,000/year from FY2026-2032)
- **CMS distributed 400 new positions in December 2025** (under CAA 2021 and CAA 2023); 135 hospitals in 37 states received positions; 62% going to primary care and psychiatry
- Since 1997 Balanced Budget Act cap, only 1,200 total new positions had been created prior to this legislation
- **If RPSRA passes:** adds ~1,400 new programs over 7 years → ~10% more scheduling software buyers
- AAMC projects **U.S. will face shortage of up to 86,000 physicians by 2036** — creating permanent pressure to expand GME

### ACGME Requirements Driving Technology Adoption
*Source: [ACGME Common Program Requirements](https://www.acgme.org/newsroom/2019/1/well-being-and-work-hour-requirements/), [ACGME Digital Transformation 2025](https://www.acgme.org/newsroom/e-communication/2025/february-10-2025/)*

Key ACGME mandates that require scheduling software:
1. **80-hour work week rule** (averaged over 4 weeks): programs must document and track; ACGME "strictly monitors and enforces" with citations for violations — strong compliance driver
2. **Duty hours tracking**: all residency programs must track and report; violations can result in probationary accreditation or loss of accreditation
3. **Milestones reporting** (twice yearly for all 167,083 residents/fellows): requires integrated GME management systems
4. **CLER (Clinical Learning Environment Review)** visits: ACGME site visits review scheduling practices, work environment, supervision
5. **ACGME Cloud (2025 launch)**: ACGME's own digital transformation is moving program data online, creating pressure on legacy programs using spreadsheets to upgrade

The 2019 ACGME flexibility update actually **increased scheduling complexity** (programs can now let residents stay past scheduled hours if schedules are built with buffer) → more administrative burden, not less.

### Trend: Cloud Migration from Legacy Systems
*Source: [Grand View Research US Healthcare Staffing & Scheduling Software Market](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/us-healthcare-staffing-scheduling-software-market-report), [Persistence Market Research 2025](https://www.persistencemarketresearch.com/market-research/physician-scheduling-systems-market.asp)*

- Cloud-based scheduling software holds **47.8% of global physician scheduling market** in 2025 (Persistence Market Research) — growing
- US healthcare staffing & scheduling software market: **$1.14B in 2024 → projected $3.12B by 2033** (CAGR 11.86%) — [Grand View Research](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/us-healthcare-staffing-scheduling-software-market-report)
- Physician scheduling systems market specifically: **$557.2M globally in 2025 → $1,318.5M by 2032** (CAGR 13.1%) — [Persistence Market Research](https://www.persistencemarketresearch.com/market-research/physician-scheduling-systems-market.asp)
- North America: ~**36.7% of global physician scheduling market** (~$204M of $557M in 2025)
- CMS's 2024 **Minimum Staffing Standards Rule** compels healthcare facilities to maintain accurate staffing records → direct driver of scheduling software adoption
- COVID-19 accelerated adoption: virtual care expansion, scheduling complexity for split in-person/telemedicine rotations, and staffing crisis awareness all drove purchasing decisions 2020-2022

### Key Strategic Observation
**The QGenda + New Innovations combination** (completed July 2025) means the market leader in physician scheduling has now merged with the market leader in residency management. This creates a near-monopoly in the high-value enterprise GME segment. A new entrant would need to compete on:
1. Lower cost (Amion's historical moat)
2. Better UX/mobile experience
3. AI-driven scheduling automation
4. Niche vertical focus (military GME, specific specialty)

## 8. Adjacent Markets

### Attending/Faculty Physician Scheduling
- All 914 sponsoring institutions also schedule attending (faculty) physicians
- Large AMCs have 500-3,000+ attending physicians per institution
- QGenda's primary market is attending physician scheduling — the larger revenue pool
- Est. 700,000 physicians using QGenda covers primarily attendings, not just residents

### Nursing & Staff Scheduling
- QGenda expanded into nursing via Schedule360 acquisition (2021)
- Nursing scheduling is **3-5x larger** by headcount than physician scheduling (nurses ~4M vs physicians ~1M in US)
- Market includes UKG, Kronos, ScheduleFly, Infor — very competitive

### GME Finance / Medicare Reimbursement
- A high-value adjacent market: teaching hospitals claim $12-14B/year from Medicare for GME
- New Innovations manages $6.2B of this; MedHub is also a player
- Finance module add-ons drive significant ACV lift ($20-50K+ annually at large AMCs)

### Military-Adjacent / VA System
- VA has ~70 affiliated medical schools and ~100,000 residents rotating through VA hospitals per year
- VA has its own scheduling challenges; Amion already has VA contracts
- Defense Health Agency is centralizing IT infrastructure — potential enterprise contracts

### International GME
- ACGME International accredits programs in Middle East, Asia; MedHub signed first international customer (Qatar) in 2010
- Smaller but growing; cultural/regulatory differences make US-built software an imperfect fit

## 9. Key Assumptions & Data Quality Notes

### What We Know with High Confidence (Primary Sources)
- ACGME program counts: **13,762 programs, 914 institutions, 167,083 trainees** (directly from ACGME annual report, January 2026)
- Military: **25 hospitals, 216 programs, ~3,000 trainees** (TRICARE newsroom, multiple military hospital sites)
- Amion pricing: **$349-449/year per group** (confirmed via their order page, enterprise PDF document, Reddit anecdotes)
- QGenda valuation: **$1.05B in 2020, $2-3B in 2024** (PE Hub, WSJ)
- M&A: PerfectServe/Lightning Bolt (2019), QGenda/Schedule360 (2021), Hearst/QGenda (2024), QGenda/New Innovations (July 2025) — all confirmed via press releases

### What Requires Triangulation / Estimation
- **QGenda ACV:** $55,000 average is derived from acquisition valuation ÷ customer count ÷ assumed revenue multiple. Actual figure not disclosed.
- **MedHub pricing:** Derived from anecdotal reports ($20K-$100K+). Officially quote-based.
- **New Innovations pricing:** Never publicly disclosed; estimated based on market position relative to MedHub.
- **Amion market share in residency programs:** "70%+ of teaching hospitals" claim from Amion's own 2017 marketing document — likely accurate but not independently verified.
- **TigerConnect per-provider pricing:** Estimated from broader TigerConnect platform pricing norms; scheduling is a module.

### Market Size Caveats
- Published market research figures ($557M-$2.1B for "physician scheduling" or "healthcare staffing & scheduling" markets) include **all physician scheduling** — not just GME/residency. The GME-specific sub-market is significantly smaller.
- The broad market figures from Persistence Market Research, Grand View, and Fortune Business Insights are credible directionally but vary widely in methodology.
- A residency-focused product competes in the **$15-125M TAM range** depending on scope; achieving QGenda-scale revenue requires expanding beyond GME into attending scheduling and workforce management.

### GSA/Government Pricing Gaps
- No public GSA schedule pricing found for residency scheduling software specifically
- Military hospital IT procurement is through DHA/DoD IT channels — not publicly accessible pricing
- Government contracts likely follow cost-plus or firm-fixed-price structures with competitive procurement
